Artificial Intelligence Was Born

view website

Speaking and taking look back technology, its advancing fast and taking less time to be widely adopted than ever before, it took roughly 10,000 years to go writing to printing press but only 500 more to get to email. Now it seems we’re at dawn of a new age - THE AGE of A.I.
The race to become an A.I superpower is on.
A.I is a branch of computer science dealing with the simulation of intelligent behaviour in computer. A.I is teaching the machine and the machine becoming smart, each time we create a more powerful technology, we create a bigger lever for changing the world, it’s an extraordinary time, one of the most unprecedented change and possibility.

Inside, the work of Mark Sagar and the University of Auckland School of Animate Technologies is being demonstrated. Sagar (CEO of soul machines and working on affective computing) who as part of the team at director Peter Jackson's Weta Digital was behind the computer-generated faces in films such as King Kong and Avatar, says Baby X is an exploration of emotional interaction through an interactive avatar.

BABY X is a life like simulation of a toddler. she can actually see through the web camera, listing through the microphone can also control the light and sound in the room.
baby x is about exploring the nature we build a digital consciousness, if possible. Mark Sagar model baby x after this own daughter, so if we start revealing her layers, she's driven by virtual muscles and virtual brain those are radically simplified models from real thing. The virtual brain driven by neural networks (neural network is a virtual much simpler version of human brain. the brain is the most complex system in our body. it’s got 85 billion neurons each of which fire non- stop, revving processing and sending information.)

Baby x brain has instead of neurons it’s got nodes, the more the nodes are exposed the more they learn.
Today most Avtar are basically glorified customer - services reps, they can answer simple questions and give scripted responses.
Baby x is a type of A.I called abject recognition, she can respond emotionally by the virtual neurotransmitter’s, hormones and so baby x has a stress system so basically some noradrenaline is released then she's gone into a much more vigilant state of mind.
Sagar describes Baby X as a kitset allowing biological components to be put together as applications.
One of the biggest misconceptions of A.I is that there is a super - intelligent being, or what we call a generalized A.I that knowns all and can do all, smarter than all of us put together. A.I is built on us, its mimicking our thought processes or u can say it’s an emulation of us.
